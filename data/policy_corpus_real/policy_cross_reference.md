This document clarifies areas where AAAI policies from different sources intersect or require nuanced interpretation. It does not introduce new policy — it explains how existing policies relate to each other.

## AI Usage vs AI Authorship

Two policies govern how AI systems relate to AAAI publications. They address different aspects and both apply simultaneously.

**Manuscript Preparation (Call for Papers):** Authors may judiciously use generative AI tools (such as large language models and image generators) in preparing their scholarly manuscripts. Authors remain fully responsible for all submitted material.

**Authorship and Citation (Publication Policies):** Any AI system does not satisfy the criteria for authorship of papers published by AAAI and cannot be used as a citable source. Attribution of authorship carries with it accountability for the work, which cannot be effectively applied to AI systems. The use of any AI system in the development of an AAAI-affiliated publication is only allowed if its role is properly documented in the manuscript.

**Combined interpretation:** Authors are permitted to use AI tools as aids during writing (e.g., improving wording, generating initial drafts that are then substantially revised). However, the AI system must not be listed as an author, must not appear as a cited source, and its use must be documented in the manuscript. The authors bear full responsibility for all content, including any AI-generated material. Any plagiarism (including unattributed AI-generated text), non-existent references, or other violations remain subject to sanctions.

## Page Limits: Submission vs Camera-Ready

Page limits differ between the submission phase and the camera-ready phase.

**At submission:** Submissions are limited to 7 pages of main content, with a maximum total length of 9 pages. Any pages beyond page 7 are reserved exclusively for references. The reproducibility checklist does not count toward the page limit and is to be submitted separately. Acknowledgements should be omitted from papers submitted for review.

**At camera-ready (after acceptance):** Accepted papers are allocated 7 pages in the proceedings plus up to 2 acknowledgement/reference pages (the reference pages can now have acknowledgements and other non-technical sections such as disclaimer of AI usage, ethical impact and statement of contribution). Authors have the option to purchase up to 2 additional pages for additional technical content at $300 per page. All references from the submitted paper must appear in the final version unless superseded.

## Reviewer Confidentiality and LLM Use

Two policies govern reviewer confidentiality. The general ethics framework and the specific LLM prohibition both apply.

**General Confidentiality (Publication Ethics and Malpractice Statement):** Reviewers must treat assigned papers and their reviews as confidential documents. Neither the papers nor the reviews may be shown to or discussed with others except as authorized by the editor, program chair, or submitting author. No information — including content, deliberations, decisions, or discussions with other reviewers — may be disclosed to outside persons.

**LLM-Specific Prohibition (Publication Policies):** Submitted papers cannot be uploaded into any system that does not ensure they are not shared with others. This includes prompting Large Language Models with papers or parts of them, because these systems may disclose part of the prompt to other users.

**Reviewer Use of Tools for Review Writing (Publication Policies):** Reviewers are fully responsible for the entire content of their reviews. Tools can be used to improve the wording of a review but not to generate its content.

**Combined interpretation:** Reviewers must not upload submission content (full papers, excerpts, figures, or data) to any LLM or external system. They may use tools (including AI tools that do not retain or share input) to polish the language of reviews they have independently written, but may not use such tools to generate the substance of the review. The confidentiality obligation covers all aspects of the submission and the review process.
